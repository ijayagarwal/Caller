the problem we are solving from first principles
The root problemfoundation
Every business needs customers. Getting customers requires someone to find leads, call them, talk to them naturally, handle their objections, follow up repeatedly, and eventually convert them. Today that "someone" is a human salesperson — expensive, inconsistent, unavailable at night, and limited to maybe 80 calls a day. A single human salesperson in India costs ₹15,000–25,000 per month plus they quit, get sick, have bad days, and forget to follow up.
What humans do in sales

Find leads → research them → call → pitch → handle objections → follow up → follow up again → close → log in CRM → repeat 80 times a day

What's broken

80 calls/day max. Miss evenings and weekends. Forget follow-ups. Inconsistent pitch. Quit after 3 months. Cost ₹20K+/month each.

What we replace

An AI that makes 1000 calls/day, never forgets, speaks Hindi/Hinglish/English, follows up automatically, works 24/7, costs ₹10K/month total.

The core loop we are buildingfoundation
Business gives us leads→ AI asks setup questions→ AI calls every lead→ Handles conversation→ Books meetings / closes→ Follows up on WhatsApp + email→ Business sees results on dashboard
That is the entire product. Every feature we build either improves one of these steps or adds a new channel. Nothing else.
Why now — why this works in 2026AI
Voice AI crossed the threshold

Cartesia and ElevenLabs now deliver voice under 100ms latency. Conversations feel human. This was not possible 18 months ago.

LLMs handle objections

Claude and GPT-4.1 can understand context, handle "I'm not interested," respond naturally, and recover the conversation. Scripted IVR couldn't do this.

Sarvam solves India

Hindi, Hinglish, Tamil, Telugu — now available via API. 600M+ non-English Indian users can now be reached with AI that speaks their language.

Infrastructure is free to start

$75K+ in credits means we can run hundreds of thousands of calls before paying a rupee. Zero financial risk to build and validate.

Module 4 — Transcript + intelligence enginedata
Every call is recorded and transcribed in real time. Each line of the transcript is tagged — who said it, when, what language, what sentiment, what intent. After the call ends, Claude reads the full transcript and extracts: what objections came up, what the lead's interest level is, what the next best action is, and what the outcome was. This data is stored structurally so you can query across all calls — "show me every call where the lead said 'too expensive'" — and fix that objection in the script immediately. Over time this becomes your proprietary dataset that trains a better model than anyone else's.

the data and AI brain — how it thinks
What the AI knows before every callAI
Before dialing, the AI loads its context: who it is calling, what it knows about them (from CSV or enrichment), what the business sells, the entire product knowledge base, the current campaign goal, the conversation flow it should follow, all objection handlers, and what outcome to target. This is fed as a structured system prompt into Claude. The AI never hallucinates product details because it only speaks from verified information the business uploaded. Langfuse traces every decision so we can see exactly why it said what it said.
Business context (RAG)

Business uploads PDFs, brochures, pricing. Embedded in vector DB. AI retrieves relevant chunks during calls to answer product questions accurately.

Lead context

Name, company, industry, any previous interactions, what channel they came from. AI personalises opening based on this.

Conversation graph

Structured flow: opener → discovery → pitch → objection handling → close → exit. AI follows this but can adapt naturally mid-conversation.

Guardrails

Hard rules: never promise what the business hasn't said it can deliver. If asked something not in knowledge base — say "let me have our team follow up on that."

Database structure — what we storedata
businesses table

ID, name, industry, onboarding answers, product knowledge, campaign config, billing plan, owner WhatsApp number.

leads table

ID, business_id, name, phone, company, source, status, call count, last_contacted, interest_score, next_action.

calls table

ID, lead_id, start time, duration, pickup (yes/no), recording URL, outcome, raw transcript, structured analysis.

transcripts table

call_id, timestamp, speaker (AI/human), text, language, sentiment, intent tag. One row per utterance.

messages table

All WhatsApp, SMS, email sent. Channel, content, sent time, open/read status, reply received.

outcomes table

meetings_booked, attended, converted to paying. Used for billing calculations and ROI reporting.

The learning loop — how it gets smarterAI
After every 100 calls, the system runs an analysis: which call openings had the best 30-second retention? Which objection handlers led to the most bookings? What time of day gets highest pickup rates in this industry? These insights automatically update the conversation flow. The AI gets measurably better every week just by running more calls. After 10,000 calls across all businesses, we fine-tune our own Llama model on Unsloth using this real Indian sales conversation data. At that point our model outperforms general-purpose LLMs for Indian sales specifically — and no competitor can replicate it because they don't have the data.
detailed task breakdown — every piece we build
1

Project setup and infrastructure

Next.js frontend. Node.js backend. Supabase database. Azure hosting. All API keys stored in Infisical from minute one. GitHub repo with CI/CD via GitHub Actions. Cursor for development speed.

Why: getting infra wrong early means rebuilding later. One day investment saves weeks.
2

Database schema — all 6 tables

Design and create: businesses, leads, calls, transcripts, messages, outcomes. Include pgvector extension for RAG. Get relationships and indexes right now — impossible to restructure with live data later.

Why: every feature depends on this. Bad schema = broken product.
3

Vapi integration — first working call

Connect Vapi to a Twilio India number. Configure Deepgram as STT. Configure ElevenLabs as TTS. Add Claude as the LLM brain. Make the AI call your own phone. It should answer, say hello, handle one question, and end gracefully.

Why: until a real call works, nothing else matters.
4

Sarvam AI for Hindi and Hinglish

Integrate Sarvam AI as alternate STT and TTS for Hindi/Hinglish calls. Build language detection — if lead speaks Hindi, switch to Sarvam automatically mid-call. Test with native Hindi speakers.

Why: 70% of your Indian market does not operate in English. This is not optional.
5

Langfuse observability — trace every LLM call

Wrap every Claude API call with Langfuse tracing. Log: input prompt, output, latency, token count, call ID, lead ID. When AI says something wrong, you find it in 2 minutes not 2 hours.

Why: hallucination on a live call destroys trust. You must be able to find and fix it instantly.
6

Business onboarding — the 7-question flow

Build a conversational onboarding where business answers 7 questions in plain text. System generates: system prompt for AI, opening script, objection handler map, campaign config. Store in businesses table. Business can edit anytime.

7

RAG knowledge base

Business uploads PDFs, Word docs, pricing sheets. System chunks them, embeds with OpenAI embeddings, stores in Supabase pgvector. During calls, AI retrieves relevant chunks to answer product questions. No hallucinations on product facts.

8

Lead upload — CSV and Google Sheets

Drag and drop CSV upload. Auto-detect columns: name, phone, email, company, notes. Validate all phone numbers to E.164 format. Remove duplicates. Show preview with row count before launching. Handle up to 100,000 rows.

9

The conversation engine — full call flow

Build the modular conversation graph: opener (10 seconds) → interest check → discovery questions → pitch → objection handler (minimum 5 objections mapped) → close attempt → graceful exit with follow-up scheduled. Test every branch path works correctly.

10

Outbound dialer with smart queue

Queue management system. Voicemail detection — if voicemail, drop a message and mark for follow-up. No-answer → retry after 2 hours, max 3 attempts. Time-aware dialing — no calls before 9am or after 7pm in lead's timezone. Concurrent call limit management.

11

Real-time transcript storage

Every utterance stored as it happens: timestamp, speaker (AI or human), text, detected language, sentiment score. Linked to call ID and lead ID. Searchable. This is the raw material for everything — analytics, fine-tuning, debugging, client reporting.

12

Post-call AI analysis

After each call ends, Claude reads the full transcript and extracts: objections raised (list), interest score 1–10, conversation outcome, recommended next action, any promises made that need follow-up. All saved to calls table. Updates lead status automatically.

13

Cal.com meeting booking integration

When AI determines a lead wants to book — it checks business owner's calendar via Cal.com API, offers 3 time slots, confirms booking, sends calendar invite to lead's email, sends WhatsApp confirmation. Meeting appears in business owner's calendar automatically.

14

Hot lead WhatsApp alert

The moment AI scores a lead 8 or above on interest — instant WhatsApp to business owner. Message includes lead's name, phone number, 3-line transcript summary, and a link to full transcript. Business owner can call back personally within minutes while lead is still warm.

15

Business dashboard — MVP version

Four views: today's summary (calls made, pickups, meetings booked), lead pipeline (all leads with statuses), transcript viewer (click any lead to see full call), analytics (conversion rates, best times, top objections). Built in Next.js with Supabase realtime for live updates.

The decision engine that decides what to do after every call and every message. Rules: no-answer → SMS 5 min → WhatsApp 1 hour → email next morning → call retry day 3. Each channel response updates the state. No lead is ever forgotten. No lead is ever over-contacted.
